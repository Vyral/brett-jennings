If you’re over 55 and own a home in the Bay Area, you’ve probably heard about Prop 19 and found yourself wondering how it affects you. Can you move without losing the low property tax benefits you’ve built up over the years? Let’s break it down so you know exactly what’s possible.

I recently worked with a couple in [Campbell](https://therealexperts.com/neighborhoods/campbell), both over 60, who had owned their home for decades. They wanted to downsize, but they were worried about giving up the super-low property taxes they’d enjoyed for the last 30 years.

After we reviewed Prop 19 together, they realized they could transfer their current property tax base to a new home, once or several times, as long as they stayed within California. By making the right move, they were able to downsize, reduce their upkeep, and still keep that low property tax bill. Here’s what you need to know.

**What Prop 19 actually does.** [Proposition 19](https://www.boe.ca.gov/prop19/) allows homeowners over 55 to transfer the allowable tax value of their current home to a new primary residence anywhere in California. In plain terms, if you have low property taxes today, you don’t have to start over with a higher property tax bill just because you move. There is one limitation worth understanding: if you buy a home that’s worth more than your current one, you’ll pay taxes on the difference in value. But it’s only on the increased value, not the whole new home.

{% include pullquote.html %}

**Who qualifies.** You qualify if you’re over 55, severely disabled, or a victim of a wildfire or natural disaster. The key requirement is that you have to be moving into a new primary residence, not a rental or a vacation home.

**How many times can you use it?** Under Prop 19, you can transfer your property tax base up to three times in your lifetime. That’s a significant benefit if you plan to downsize now and think you might move again later.

**Why planning and timing matter so much.** Timing is key here. You want to coordinate your move carefully so you get the most out of the tax benefit, and this is where having the right guidance pays off. Working with a real estate expert who truly understands Prop 19 helps make sure you don’t accidentally lose this advantage or trigger a higher tax bill than you need to.

Prop 19 is a game-changer for Bay Area homeowners over 55 who want to move, downsize, or relocate somewhere else in California. It lets you take your low property tax benefits with you, potentially multiple times, while giving you flexibility for your next chapter.
