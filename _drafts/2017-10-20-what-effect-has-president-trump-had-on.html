Now that we’re into July, President Trump has officially been in office for six months. What effect has his tenure had on the real estate market?

So far, his policies are supporting the real estate market, for a couple key reasons.

First, his tax plan is preserving the mortgage interest deduction. For all of us Bay Area homeowners who have large mortgages, that’s a major win. He’s also increasing the standard deduction by doubling it. This will effectively give more discretionary income to homebuyers and make homeownership more affordable because, up until this time, we reached somewhat of a ceiling in terms of affordability last summer.

Now is a good time to buy and sell in our market.

Second, his pro-business policies have boosted the stock market by 17% since the day he was elected. This is pushing home prices higher for the simple reason that the majority of homeowners and homebuyers in Silicon Valley have some sort of employee stock purchase plan. They’ve got a good amount of wealth when buying a home, and they can cash in some of their stock to buy that home. As long as our stock market continues on this trend, we can expect prices to continue to rise.

If you’re considering buying, I wouldn’t wait on the sidelines. Interest rates are at all-time lows, and as the stock market trends higher and higher, it will make homes more expensive. If you’re considering selling, now might be the time to do so because the market is at an all-time high— and we don’t know how much longer that will last.
