Understanding the real estate market is not all that complicated. The market is driven by supply and demand, and it's tempered by affordability and interest rates. 

Before we look ahead, let's briefly look back at what happened in 2014. Every single market in our area (Los Gatos, Saratoga, Willow Glen, Cupertino, Los Altos, Mountain View, Palo Alto, San Jose) saw more than 10% rises in growth, but those numbers are actually down from recent years because of rising home prices and declining affordability. 

For a good real estate market, you need a good economy and lots of new jobs. In fact, Silicon Valley added nearly 47,000 jobs in 2013, and 30,000 people migrated here for those jobs, creating a large demand for housing. The key here is that only 8,000 new units were built, and the supply is not meeting the demand. Silicon Valley only has 4-6 weeks of inventory, as opposed to a healthy market where 4-6 months of inventory is the norm. 

Demand in 2015 will be driven by job growth, and companies like Apple and Google are buying enormous new office spaces, which is a good sign for our real estate market. 

Supply will be lacking in 2015, with no increases in the foreseeable future. Builders cannot construct homes quick enough to keep up with the demand. This will inevitably drive prices up, but I don't think we're headed towards a bubble.

I say this because as prices increase, affordability decreases. In 2012, nearly 50% of consumers could afford to buy a home, and now in 2014, only 25% of buyers could afford homes. In 2015 I expect the rate at which prices increase to slow down, but affordability will continue to decrease.

Interest rates may hit the 5% mark by 2015, making this the year to move up into a bigger home. The cost of housing is only expected to rise, so this is a great time to take advantage of the market in San Jose. If you lock in a low rate now, you have the chance to save hundreds of thousands of dollars.
